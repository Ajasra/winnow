Co-constitution is the [[Karen-Barad]] principle that individuals do not preexist their interaction. Boundaries are drawn during the act of relating — they are not pre-set. The person does not preexist before interacting with the work; both emerge together through their encounter.

This contradicts the idea of a "curator of conditions" who stands outside the system. From outside, a person with a spoon appears separate from the tool. But holding a pen, the pen feels like part of the self. The [[agential-cut]] is at the interface between components of the phenomenon, not at a pre-given boundary between subject and object.

Co-constitution connects to [[intra-action]] (agencies emerge through their entanglement, not prior to it), to [[onto-epistemology]] (being and knowing are produced together), and to [[performativity]] (identity is enacted, not given). It also connects to [[second-order-cybernetics]]: the observer is part of the system, not external to it.
