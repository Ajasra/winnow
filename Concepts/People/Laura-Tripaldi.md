Laura Tripaldi is a materials scientist and theorist of new materialism. Her work examines the intersection of materiality, intelligence, and embodiment.

Tripaldi's core contribution is [[technomorphism]]: comparing body/mind to machines also sets limits on those machines. The form of thinking comes from the form of actions, and those actions are based on affordance and constraints. Transformation and deformation of substrate is not a technical issue — it is the foundation of intelligent behavior.

She defines responsibility as agency + foresight + self-reflection + ability to imagine different outcomes. Inheritance, in her framework, is defined by who and what intra-acts. Intelligence is a scar — memory, experiences made material. The scar is not possible without materiality and embodiment. Using scar as metaphor limits the scope of intelligence.

Her work connects to [[kintsugi]], [[scars-of-reality]], and [[cognitive-homeostasis]].
