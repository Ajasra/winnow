The agential cut is a central concept in [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-realism]]. An apparatus is a boundary-making practice that creates an affordance space. The cut enacts a local separation (subject/object) within global entanglement — it is not given in advance but performed.

Every cut closes some futures and opens others. The location of the cut shifts with the apparatus: holding a pen places the cut at the nib/paper interface, not at the hand/pen. The cut creates stability and reproducibility by momentarily fixing what is otherwise fluid.

This concept connects to [[intra-action]] (agencies do not precede the cut but emerge through it), to [[co-constitution]] (individuals do not preexist their interaction), to [[space-time-mattering]] (every cut creates matter, space, and time simultaneously), and to [[ethics-of-mattering]] (we are accountable for what the cut excludes).
