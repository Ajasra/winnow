Structural coupling is a core concept in [[Maturana-Varela]]'s theory of [[autopoiesis]]. It describes a history of mutual congruent structural changes between a unity and its environment, lasting as long as both maintain their organization without disintegration.

The environment triggers structural changes in autopoietic unities but does not specify or direct them — it perturbs, but the system determines its own response. This preserves operational closure while allowing adaptation. The nervous system is structurally coupled to the organism through sensory and effector surfaces, not through information transfer.

Structural coupling underlies the rejection of the [[tube-metaphor-is-false|tube metaphor]]: communication depends on what happens to the receiver, not on what is transmitted. Connects to [[ontology]] (history of structural change without loss of organization), [[second-order-cybernetics]], and [[agential-realism]].
