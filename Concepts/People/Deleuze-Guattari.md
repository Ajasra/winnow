Gilles Deleuze (1925–1995) and Félix Guattari (1930–1992) were French philosophers whose collaborative works, particularly *Anti-Oedipus*, developed a machinic ontology with direct relevance to cybernetics and autopoietic systems.

Two key concepts emerge from their work. The [[machinic-phylum]] is not simply tools and machines but a dynamic network of technologies and people — a social machine operating at the societal level. [[Desiring-machines]] describe the capacity for an unlimited number of connections, in every sense and in all directions, breaking down the distinction between the natural and the artificial.

In their framework, individuals are discrete [[autopoiesis|autopoieses]] engaging in feedback processes, integrating interests, intentions, and skills into a desiring system. This connects their thought to [[Gordon-Pask]]'s Conversation Theory and to the [[intra-action]] framework of [[Karen-Barad]].
