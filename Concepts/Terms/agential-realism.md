Agential realism is Karen Barad's onto-epistemological framework, developed in *Meeting the Universe Halfway*. It rejects the observer/observed split entrenched in representationalist science. Phenomena, not independent objects or subjects, are the primary ontological units.

The framework rests on [[intra-action]] rather than interaction. Where interaction presumes pre-existing relata that then act upon each other, intra-action holds that relata emerge through the relation itself. An [[agential-cut]], a boundary-making practice, creates local separation within global entanglement, producing subject and object in the same gesture. Every cut simultaneously creates matter as boundary, space as separability, and time as causality.

This aligns with [[second-order-cybernetics]] in rejecting the position of an outside observer. Barad extends this further into [[onto-epistemology]]: knowing is intra-acting, and intra-acting brings new realities into being. Causality in agential realism is a handshake: the shake creates the shaker. Past constrains future, but every cut reconfigures possibilities for the next cut. [[agency-is-verb-not-noun|Agency]] is not control but participation in the world's ongoing reconfiguration, guided by [[ethics-of-mattering]]: accountability for what the cut includes and excludes. [[space-time-mattering]] describes how every cut produces matter, space, and time simultaneously. [[diffraction]] provides the methodology: how phenomena interfere with and reconfigure each other. [[performativity]] insists that descriptions are acts that shape what they describe. [[co-constitution]] holds that entities do not preexist their relations.
