Wabi-sabi (侘寂) is a Japanese aesthetic worldview centered on the acceptance of transience and incompleteness. Beauty resides in that which is imperfect, impermanent, and incomplete.

Wabi refers to rustic simplicity, quietness, and the beauty of the understated. Sabi refers to the beauty that comes with age — the patina of use, the wear of time. Together they describe an aesthetic that values what is modest, weathered, and passing over what is pristine, permanent, and perfect.

Wabi-sabi grounds [[kintsugi]] (the gold-repaired ceramic celebrates imperfection rather than concealing it) and connects to [[mono-no-aware]] (the pathos of transience), to [[mottainai]] (regret over waste, respect for intrinsic value), and to [[scars-of-reality]] (identity formed through visible marks rather than erased errors).
