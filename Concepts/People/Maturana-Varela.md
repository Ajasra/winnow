Humberto Maturana and Francisco Varela developed the theory of [[autopoiesis]], self-producing and self-maintaining systems, in their collaborative works *The Tree of Knowledge* and *Autopoiesis and Cognition*. Their foundational theorem: "Anything said is said by an observer." Every act of indicating involves an act of distinction, separating what is indicated from its background.

Autopoiesis describes systems whose primary goal is maintenance of their own organization. The environment triggers structural changes but does not specify or direct them. Structural coupling names the history of mutual congruent structural changes between unity and environment, sustained as long as neither disintegrates. Ontogeny is the history of structural change in a unity without loss of organization, ceasing only with disintegration.

They distinguished human social systems from biological organisms: organisms restrict individual creativity of their components, while human social systems amplify it through the linguistic domain that emerges from structural coupling. Their rejection of the tube metaphor, where communication depends on what happens to the receiver, directly challenges [[Claude-Shannon]]'s transmission model.

Maturana and Varela's work grounds [[second-order-cybernetics]] (the observer cannot stand outside), [[symbiomemesis]] (systems that maintain organization through perturbation), [[agential-realism]] (the inseparability of observer and phenomenon), and [[intra-action]] (structural coupling as mutual co-constitution without pre-existing relata).
