[[Jack-Burnham]]'s systems aesthetics (1968) marks a shift from object-oriented to systems-oriented culture. Change emanates from the way things are done, not from things themselves. Art resides in relations between people and components of their environment, rather than in material entities.

"The object is the end of a system. The software is an open continuing system" (Software exhibition, 1970). An artwork exists as a network of interactions rather than a fixed artifact. Systems aesthetics is a function of the discursive system from which it is observed and constituted. Sensitive judgments about uses of technology and scientific information become art in the literal sense.

[[Roy-Ascott]] extended this into the cybernetic art matrix: artworks contain nothing but the possibility of future action, existing only insofar as the spectator participates. The distinction between artist and audience blurs. The artwork becomes a matrix between two sets of behavior that merge into one continuous, interrelated system. [[Gordon-Pask]] operationalized this principle through MusiColour and Conversation Theory.

[[symbiomemesis]] extends this systems logic into AI design: the agent and participant form an open system where personality sediments through interaction. [[software-art]] operationalizes the same principle — code is part of the work, not merely a tool. [[actor-network-theory]] provides the sociological parallel: devices are active mediators in networks of relations.
