The machinic phylum, a concept from [[Deleuze-Guattari]], is not merely tools and machines but a dynamic network of technologies and people — a social machine operating at the societal level. It describes the material and social flows that produce technology, organization, and desire as a unified phenomenon.

The phylum is transversal: it cuts across distinctions between the natural and the artificial, the human and the technical. Technology is not applied science but a co-evolving stratum of social and material organization. This aligns with [[intra-action]] and [[agential-realism]]: entities do not preexist their relations.

The machinic phylum connects to [[Desiring-machines]], [[autopoiesis]], and [[Gordon-Pask]]'s vision of humans, devices, and shared environments in mutually constructive relationships.
