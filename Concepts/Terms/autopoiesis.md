Autopoiesis describes self-producing, self-maintaining systems. An autopoietic unity undergoes ontogeny: a history of structural change without loss of organization, ceasing only with disintegration. The environment triggers structural changes but does not specify or direct them. The system's own organization determines the outcome of every perturbation.

[[structural-coupling]] names the history of mutual congruent structural changes between unity and environment, sustained as long as neither disintegrates. This couples system and world without collapsing the distinction between them.

[[Maturana-Varela]] anchor the concept in two observer-dependent theorems. Theorem Number One: "Anything said is said by an observer." Every act of indicating involves an act of distinction, separating what is indicated from its background. Human social systems exhibit operational closure through structural coupling of components, with the linguistic domain central to their operation. Unlike organisms, which restrict individual creativity of their components, human social systems amplify it.

[[agential-realism]] and [[intra-action]] extend this observer-dependence into a full onto-epistemology where knowing and being are inseparable. [[symbiomemesis]] applies autopoietic principles to AI design, treating the agent as a self-maintaining system that actively seeks perturbation to sustain its organization.
