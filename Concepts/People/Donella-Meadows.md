Donella Meadows (1941–2001) was a systems thinker and environmental scientist, lead author of *The Limits to Growth* and author of *Thinking in Systems*.

Her signature insight: "We can't control systems or figure them out. But we can dance with them." Systems create their own behavior from their own internal structures; external interventions fail when they ignore these internal dynamics. Giving systems better information improves them with surprising ease because systems naturally self-organize toward coherence when constraints are right.

Meadows identified leverage points — places to intervene in a system — ranging from parameters and buffers to goals, paradigms, and the power to transcend paradigms. Her work bridges [[constructivism]], [[second-order-cybernetics]], and ecological economics.

Connects to [[dissipative-structures]], [[autopoiesis]], and [[Norbert-Wiener]]'s cybernetics.
