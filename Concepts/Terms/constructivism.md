Constructivism is the epistemological position that we do not merely discover laws of nature — we construct our own realities. What we observe is not nature itself but nature exposed to our method of questioning, as Heisenberg formulated. Knowledge is not a representation of an independent world but a construction by the observer.

This is the foundational epistemology of [[second-order-cybernetics]]: the observer cannot stand outside the observed, and every description is an intervention. The shift from objective to epistemic science, from what is true to how we know, is the third of five paradigm shifts in systems thinking.

Constructivism connects to [[agential-realism]] and [[onto-epistemology]] (Barad's "knowing is making"), to [[Maturana-Varela]]'s theorem that "anything said is said by an observer," and to [[Mary-Catherine-Bateson]]'s insight that "each person is his own central metaphor."
