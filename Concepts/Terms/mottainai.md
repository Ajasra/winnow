Mottainai (勿体無い) is a Japanese concept expressing regret over waste and respect for the intrinsic value of objects. It carries a sense of "too good to waste" — a moral imperative against squandering what has value, whether material or immaterial.

The concept grounds the ethics of [[kintsugi]]: broken things are not less valuable — they are more, precisely because they carry history. To discard a broken object is mottainai; to repair it with gold is to honor its value. Mottainai extends beyond objects to time, effort, and potential.

Connects to [[wabi-sabi]] (imperfection as value, not defect), to [[mono-no-aware]] (appreciation of the passing), and to [[ethics-of-mattering]] (accountability for what is included and excluded in every material practice).
