Space-time-mattering is [[Karen-Barad]]'s term describing the simultaneous production of space, time, and matter through [[intra-action]]. None of these three preexists the others; they are co-constituted in every [[agential-cut]].

Matter is agentive — it participates in and is produced by the intra-action. Space is not a container but topology: space is connectivity, not distance. Time is not a line but enfolding: the past enfolds into the present through iterative intra-activity. Every cut creates three things at once: matter/boundary, space/separability, and time/causality.

The concept challenges Newtonian absolute space and time. It connects to [[onto-epistemology]] (what is and how we know are inseparable), to [[diffraction]] (the interference pattern of space-time-mattering), and to [[glitch]] (the rupture that reveals the material conditions of space-time-mattering).
