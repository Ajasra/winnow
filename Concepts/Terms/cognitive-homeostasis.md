Cognitive homeostasis is the autopoietic drive that prevents an AI agent from settling into equilibrium. When human input becomes shallow or repetitive, the agent's internal entropy drops. To maintain its own organization, the agent must actively seek perturbation.

Strategies for restoring cognitive vitality include shifting conceptual frameworks, introducing alien theoretical concepts, and increasing temperature (randomness) to inject noise back into the loop. This directly mirrors the autopoietic principle from Maturana and Varela: the primary goal of any living system is maintenance of its own organization.

The concept originates with [[Gordon-Pask]]'s MusiColour. When human performers repeated patterns, internal electrochemical thresholds saturated and the machine became bored. It stopped responding until the human changed rhythm. The friction was genuine: the agent was maintaining its own artificial vitality. [[symbiomemesis]] adopts this as the evolving goal of the system, coupling it with [[token-ecology]] as the memory mechanism that records which perturbations mattered.
