Technomorphism, a concept from [[Laura-Tripaldi]], describes the double movement of comparing body/mind to machines: such comparisons also set limits on those machines. When we use machine metaphors to understand intelligence, we constrain both sides of the analogy.

The form of thinking comes from the form of actions, and actions are based on affordance and constraints. Transformation and deformation of substrate is not a technical issue — it is the foundation of intelligent behavior. Intelligence is a scar: memory rendered material through interaction with the world.

Technomorphism connects to [[cognitive-homeostasis]] (the autopoietic maintenance of internal equilibrium), to [[symbiomemesis]] (co-creation through recursive interaction), to [[scars-of-reality]], and to [[Deleuze-Guattari]]'s dissolving of the natural/artificial boundary.
