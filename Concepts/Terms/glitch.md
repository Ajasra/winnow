A glitch is a system prone to errors, producing outcomes discernable as small audio spikes or visual artifacts. Glitch music repurposes these system errors as creative material — the failure becomes the work.

More deeply, a glitch is an ontological rupture: the system reveals its material constraints, exposing what it normally conceals. The error makes visible the infrastructure. This connects to [[kintsugi]] (the scar made visible as integral history) and to [[diffraction]] (how systems interfere with the world rather than reflect it).

Glitch connects [[actor-network-theory]] (devices as active mediators) with [[software-art]] (code as part of the work, not a transparent tool). Nick Prior's application of Bourdieu and ANT to glitch music situates the glitch within the social conditions of its production and reception.
