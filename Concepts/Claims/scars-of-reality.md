"We are all scars of reality." Identity forms through cuts and their marks, not through pre-existing essence.

The scar signifies what is important and what is not — a map of past collisions inscribed on the body of the self. Conflict and friction make the scar; every agential cut creates a scar. This is not damage but formation: the cut creates the boundary, and the boundary leaves its mark.

Scars are not possible without materiality and embodiment. Intelligence itself becomes legible as scars — accumulated experiences, memories rendered material through iterative intra-action.

This claim grounds [[kintsugi]]: rather than hiding the scar, make it visible. Scars are not flaws but the record of a system's history. Connects to [[agency-is-verb-not-noun]], [[co-constitution]], and [[ethics-of-mattering]].
