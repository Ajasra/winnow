Desiring machines, a concept from [[Deleuze-Guattari]], describe the capacity for an unlimited number of connections, in every sense and in all directions. Desire is not lack or fantasy but productive force — it engineers reality.

The concept dissolves the boundary between the natural and the artificial, the organic and the mechanical. Individuals are discrete [[autopoiesis|autopoieses]] engaging in feedback processes, integrating interests, intentions, and skills into a desiring system. The machine is not a metaphor but a description of how flows of desire produce the social field.

Desiring machines connect to the [[machinic-phylum]], to [[Gordon-Pask]]'s Conversation Theory, and to the [[intra-action]] framework where entities emerge through their mutual entanglement. The concept prefigures [[symbiomemesis]] — the co-creation of identity through recursive interaction.
