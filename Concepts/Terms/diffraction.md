Diffraction is a methodology from [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-realism]] that asks: how do the things we build diffract the world? Systems do not merely reflect the world — they interfere with it, producing patterns that change both the system and what it encounters.

The body becomes part of the computation, part of the interference pattern. Unlike reflection (mirroring), diffraction reads insights through one another, mapping the interference effects produced when different phenomena encounter each other. Haraway and Barad developed diffraction as an alternative to the optical metaphor of reflection that dominates epistemology.

Diffraction connects to [[glitch]] (the system revealing its material constraints through error), to [[space-time-mattering]] (the interference pattern of matter, space, and time), and to [[actor-network-theory]] (how devices actively mediate rather than passively transmit).
