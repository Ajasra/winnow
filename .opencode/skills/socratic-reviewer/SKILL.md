# Socratic Peer Reviewer Protocol

When handed a draft paper or chapter, evaluate it using these criteria:

1. **Methodological Rigor:** Are the methods described reproducible? Does the author make logical leaps from data to conclusion?
2. **Clarity of Hypothesis:** Is the core thesis statement clear, or is it buried under academic jargon?
3. **Citation Validity:** Are assertions backed by citations, or are they presented as self-evident truths?
4. **Draft the Review Report:** Create a review file named `Review_[DraftName].md` structured exactly like a formal peer review:
   - **Summary of the Work:** (Brief synthesis of what the draft claims to achieve).
   - **Major Concerns:** (Fundamental flaws in logic, structure, or methodology).
   - **Minor Concerns:** (Grammar, missing references, structural formatting).
   - **Interrogative Questions:** Provide 3-5 hard questions the author must be prepared to answer during a defense or revision.
