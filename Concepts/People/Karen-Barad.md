Karen Barad is a feminist theorist and physicist whose book *Meeting the Universe Halfway* synthesized quantum field theory with poststructuralist thought to develop agential realism. Her central move: Bohr's quantum physics describes how reality operates at all scales.

Barad's key contributions include [[agential-realism]] (the overarching framework), [[intra-action]] (the rejection of pre-existing relata in favor of mutual constitution), the [[agential-cut]] (boundary-making practice that produces subject/object), [[onto-epistemology]] (the inseparability of knowing and being), and [[ethics-of-mattering]] (accountability for what the cut excludes). She reconceptualized causality as iterative intra-activity: every cut closes some futures and opens others. [[agency-is-verb-not-noun|Agency]], in her framework, is not a possession but the capacity to participate in the world's ongoing reconfiguration. Responsibility is the ability to respond to specific entanglements.

Barad's work converges with [[second-order-cybernetics]] in refusing the observer/observed split. Both she and Heinz von Foerster insist that describing the world changes it. Her framework aligns with [[Gregory-Bateson]]'s cybernetic epistemology, where mind and nature form a single circuit, and with [[autopoiesis]], where observer and observed are structurally coupled.
