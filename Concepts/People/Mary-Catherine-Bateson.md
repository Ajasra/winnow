Mary Catherine Bateson (1939–2021) was a cultural anthropologist, daughter of [[Gregory-Bateson]] and Margaret Mead. Her work extends cybernetic thinking into the study of selfhood, narrative, and social systems.

Her central aphorism: "Each person is his own central metaphor." This crystallizes the [[second-order-cybernetics]] principle that the observer is inextricably part of the observation — what we observe is structured by who we are. Her book *Our Own Metaphor* explores cybernetics and selfhood through the interdisciplinary lens of a conference on the subject.

Bateson's anthropology treats identity as a recursive process rather than a fixed substance, aligning with [[constructivism]] and [[autopoiesis]]. Her work connects [[Gregory-Bateson]]'s "difference that makes a difference" to lived human experience.
