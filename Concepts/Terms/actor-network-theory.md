Actor-Network Theory (ANT) is a sociological framework, primarily associated with Bruno Latour, Michel Callon, and John Law, that treats objects and non-human entities as active participants in social networks. Devices are not passive intermediaries but active mediators — they transform meaning and action rather than merely transporting them.

ANT rejects the nature/society divide: everything exists in networks of relations where agency is distributed across human and non-human actors. Nick Prior applied ANT to [[glitch]] music, using the framework to account for technology's constitutive role in creative practice.

ANT connects to [[agential-realism]]'s treatment of matter as agentive, to [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-cut]], and to [[systems-aesthetics]]'s view that art resides in relations between people and components of their environment.
