Ethics of mattering is a concept from [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-realism]]. Matter is not a thing — matter is doing. Mattering carries a double meaning: substance/meaning and importance. To matter is to materialize, and to matter is to count.

You create conditions for what and who gets to matter. You are responsible for the marks your system leaves on bodies. Responsibility is not about blame but about the capacity to respond to specific entanglements. We are accountable for what is excluded by every [[agential-cut]], not just for what is included.

This concept connects to [[diffraction]] (how systems interfere with the world), to [[scars-of-reality]] (the cuts that form identity), and to [[kintsugi]] (the ethics of making the scar visible rather than erasing it). Agency is not control; agency is participation, and participation carries ethical weight.
