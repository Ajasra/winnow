Intra-action is Karen Barad's term for the mutual constitution of entangled agencies. It differs critically from interaction, which presumes pre-existing entities that then act upon each other. Intra-action holds that entities do not preexist their relations: they emerge through them.

An [[agential-cut]] is the mechanism of intra-action. The cut is a boundary-making practice that produces determinate entities within phenomena. Subject and object are produced in the same movement. The apparatus, whether a scientific instrument, a work of art, or a social configuration, enacts the cut. Different apparatuses produce different cuts and different realities.

Intra-action operates iteratively. Each cut reconfigures possibilities for the next cut, so the future is unmade and past enfolds into present. This is not relativism but contingency: things happened this way but could have been different. [[agential-realism]] is the broader framework; intra-action is its core dynamic.

The concept shares ground with [[autopoiesis]]: structural coupling describes how system and environment co-determine each other's changes without either specifying the other. Both frameworks reject the conduit model of communication ([[tube-metaphor-is-false]]). [[Karen-Barad]] developed intra-action as part of her synthesis of quantum physics and feminist theory.
