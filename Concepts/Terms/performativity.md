Performativity, as developed in [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-realism]], is the principle that truth leaves a mark on the world through enactment. Each agent enacts specific boundaries, defining meaning through material arrangement.

Drawing from J.L. Austin (performative utterances) and Judith Butler (gender performativity), Barad extends performativity beyond language into matter itself. Descriptions are not neutral observations — they are acts that shape the world. Boundaries constrain bodies and force specific behaviors, but these boundaries are performed, not given.

Performativity connects to [[agency-is-verb-not-noun]] (agency is doing, not possession), to [[agential-cut]] (the cut is performed, not pre-existing), and to [[onto-epistemology]] (knowing is doing). Statements do not simply describe; they enact what they describe.
