Kintsugi (金継ぎ) is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold lacquer. Rather than hiding the cracks, kintsugi makes them visible, transforming damage into the object's most valued feature. The break becomes part of the object's story, giving it a new identity shaped by history.

Two aesthetic principles anchor the practice. [[wabi-sabi]] values imperfection, impermanence, and simplicity. [[mottainai]] expresses regret over waste and respect for the intrinsic value of objects. Together they invert the usual relationship between damage and worth: broken things carry more value, not less, because they contain a history that the unbroken object lacks.

In the context of [[symbiomemesis]], kintsugi names the principle by which the agent's history becomes its identity. The Kintsugi vector space is the agent's non-reversible memory: past perturbations permanently reshape internal state. Every [[token-ecology|semantic knot]], every misunderstanding and repair, leaves a trace. These traces are the material of personality. [[Laura-Tripaldi]] extends this logic: transformation and deformation of substrate is the foundation of intelligent behavior. Intelligence itself is a [[scars-of-reality|scar]].
