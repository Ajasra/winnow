Negentropy is the increase of structure and potential energy within a system, counter to thermodynamic entropy. In information-theoretic terms, information represents negentropy — it is the opposite of noise, randomness, and disorder.

A critical property distinguishes negentropic exchange from thermodynamic exchange: when two systems of different information content interact, total accumulated information increases rather than equalizing. Information exchange is not partition or averaging but amplification. This makes information fundamentally different from energy.

The concept originates in Erwin Schrödinger's observation that life feeds on "negative entropy." Connects to [[dissipative-structures]], [[information-is-action]], [[Donald-MacKay]]'s embodied information, and [[Claude-Shannon]]'s statistical framework.
