Agency is not a property possessed by entities but a capacity enacted through relations. There is no Agent as noun, only Agency as verb. Agency is the capacity for the world to reconfigure itself.

This claim draws from [[Karen-Barad]]'s [[agential-realism]] and carries implications for artificial and artistic systems. An artwork or AI system can have agency: the capacity for effect, the ability to make an [[agential-cut]], the power to reconfigure the situation. This requires refusing to treat non-human actors as passive intermediaries.

Barad's formulation resists hierarchy. Verbs have no hierarchy. Agency requires neither intention nor consciousness, only the capacity to participate in the world's ongoing reconfiguration. This mirrors [[information-is-action]] (MacKay's verb-like information) and rejects the [[tube-metaphor-is-false|tube metaphor]] of communication. Meaning is enacted at reception; agency is enacted through relation. Neither is something you have. Both are something you do.
