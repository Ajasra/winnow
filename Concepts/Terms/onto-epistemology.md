Onto-epistemology is [[Karen-Barad]]'s term for the inseparability of ontology (what is) from epistemology (how we know). You cannot know without intra-acting; knowing is changing. Knowing = Making.

This rejects the traditional philosophical separation in which being exists independently and knowledge describes it from outside. Intra-acting brings new realities into being — observation is intervention. The framework is the core methodological commitment of [[agential-realism]].

Onto-epistemology connects to [[constructivism]] (we construct rather than discover realities), to [[second-order-cybernetics]] (the observer is part of the system), and to [[Maturana-Varela]]'s theorem that "anything said is said by an observer." It also underlies [[agential-cut]]: the cut is simultaneously an ontological operation (it separates what is) and an epistemological operation (it determines what can be known).
