Software art is a process-oriented practice where code is part of the work, not merely a tool to assist interpretation. The distinction collapses: the software is the art, and the art is the software.

Abstract generative art performs cosmogony — it brings forth an artificial world, declaring "here is my world, and here's how it works." Software shows a way out of order, in and through order. The medium is reflexive: the state of the system at one moment is a function of its state in the moment just passed. History is not absent; it is encoded in the program's execution trace.

Software art connects to [[systems-aesthetics]] (Burnham's shift from object to system), to [[Roy-Ascott]]'s cybernetic art matrix (art as possibility of future action), and to [[Gordon-Pask]]'s MusiColour (machines that develop internal states through interaction).
